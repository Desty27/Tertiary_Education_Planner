Understanding Place Value in Large Numbers

Mathematics | Key Stage 2 | Year 5 | 60 minutes

Learning Objectives

Students will be able to read, write, order and compare numbers to at least 1,000,000 and determine the value of each digit.

Activities

Warm-up: Place Value Recap
Begin with a quick review of place value for numbers up to 10,000. Use place value charts and ask students to identify the value of digits in different positions.
10 minutes
Introduction to Larger Numbers
Introduce numbers up to 1,000,000 using visual aids and real-world examples (e.g., populations of cities, attendance at major events). Demonstrate how to read and write these numbers.
15 minutes
Place Value Partners
In pairs, students create place value charts for 6-digit numbers. One student calls out a number, and their partner must correctly place digits in the chart and explain the value of each digit.
15 minutes
Ordering Challenge
Provide groups with sets of cards showing 6-digit numbers. Students must arrange them in ascending or descending order and explain their reasoning.
15 minutes
Reflection and Assessment
Students complete a short assessment worksheet with questions on reading, writing, and comparing large numbers. Review answers as a class.
5 minutes

Resources

  • Place value charts
  • Number cards (0-9)
  • Large number cards for ordering activity
  • Assessment worksheet
  • Visual aids showing real-world examples of large numbers

Assessment

Formative assessment through observation and questioning during activities. Summative assessment through end-of-lesson worksheet.

Differentiation

Support: Provide place value charts with headings. Use smaller numbers for students who need additional support.

Extension: Challenge students to work with numbers beyond 1,000,000 or to solve problems involving negative numbers.
